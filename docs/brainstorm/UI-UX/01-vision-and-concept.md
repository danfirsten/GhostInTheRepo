# Ghost in the Repo — Vision & Concept

## The Premise

**Ghost in the Repo** is a mastery platform for software engineers. It teaches everything — from bit manipulation to distributed consensus, from TCP handshakes to transformer architectures — with one goal: make you *unreasonably good* at computer science.

The name carries real meaning. A ghost in the repo is someone who:
- Moves invisibly through complex systems because they *understand* them
- Leaves no trace because everything they write is obvious, clean, inevitable
- Is haunting in their competence — others wonder how they know what they know

The platform embodies that feeling. Learning here should feel like initiation into something secret and powerful. Not intimidating — the opposite. The more you learn, the more *obvious* everything becomes. Like the ghost, you stop fighting the machine. You become it.

---

## Aesthetic Direction

### Core Identity: **"Dark Academia Meets Terminal Ghost"**

Not hackery. Not matrix green on black. Not another dev tool with purple gradients.

Think:
- A beautifully typeset technical tome, but alive on screen
- The quiet power of a dark room with a single focused light source
- Something between an MIT lecture hall at midnight and a beautifully designed science journal
- Translucent, layered, spectral — like something that exists between worlds

The ghost metaphor shapes every visual decision:
- **Translucency** — frosted glass, layered surfaces, things seen through things
- **Presence without weight** — generous whitespace, floating elements, nothing feels heavy
- **Reveal** — information materializes, doesn't dump; content drifts into existence
- **The void** — deep dark backgrounds that feel infinite, not oppressive

---

## Emotional Arc

When a user visits Ghost in the Repo, they should feel this sequence:

```
1. Intrigue        → "This is unlike anything I've seen for CS learning"
2. Orientation     → "I can immediately find what I need"
3. Absorption      → "This content is genuinely excellent and easy to read"
4. Power           → "I just internalized something hard in 10 minutes"
5. Obsession       → "I'm coming back tomorrow"
```

The design must serve all five stages. The homepage triggers intrigue. The navigation triggers orientation. The typography and layout trigger absorption. The content quality + progress system triggers power. The whole experience triggers obsession.

---

## The Audience

A user who:
- Takes their craft seriously
- Is tired of tutorial hell and wants *depth*
- Wants to understand systems, not just use them
- Appreciates quality — in code and in the tools they use
- Feels the pull of mastery as an end in itself

They know what good UI looks like. They use Linear, Vercel, Raycast, Warp. They have opinions about fonts. The design must be *worthy of them*.

## Name & Logo

**Ghost** — the logo is a minimal ghost silhouette, rendered with:
- A single continuous SVG path
- Subtle gradient from pale lavender to ghostly white
- Soft, pulsing glow on hover
- The ghost's "eyes" are two small dots that animate open on page load — as if it's awakening

The ghost should feel:
- Friendly, not spooky
- Modern, not cartoonish
- Iconic — recognizable at 16px favicon size

The tagline sits beneath the logo in small caps, tracked wide:
> **"KNOW THE MACHINE. HAUNT IT."**

---

## What Makes This Unforgettable

1. **The Ghost mascot that responds to your progress** — as you complete more topics, the ghost in the sidebar becomes more "solid," more detailed, more present. At the start it's a wispy outline; at max level it glows.

2. **The Knowledge Graph** — the /paths page renders as an interactive node graph (a constellation), not a boring course list. Mastered nodes glow. Locked nodes are dim wisps.

3. **The Cheatsheet aesthetic** — each cheatsheet feels like a beautifully printed reference card, not a dump of bullet points. Typography, spacing, and syntax highlighting are obsessively refined.

4. **The "Crack Level" system** — a playful but motivating progress metric. Not gamification for its own sake — a genuine measure of depth across all topics. Called "Spectral Density" internally.

5. **Zero clutter** — no ads, no pop-ups, no "subscribe to our newsletter" banners. Just the knowledge, beautifully presented.
